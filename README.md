## ⚙️ Understanding the Features

The application identifies your pupil movement to determine your gaze. It uses internal models to translate image data into screen coordinates. 

* Visual Feedback: The red dot moves across your display to match your focus.
* Real-time Processing: The software matches your gaze movement as you look at different windows.
* Privacy: All data processing happens on your local device. The software does not send video or eye data to any server.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, review these common solutions.

If the camera fails to open:
Ensure no other application uses your webcam. Close software like Zoom, Teams, or Skype before you start. Check your Windows privacy settings to confirm that desktop apps have permission to access the camera hardware.

If the red dot stays in one place:
Check the lighting in your room. Avoid sitting with a bright window behind you, as this creates glare and makes it hard for the sensor to see your eyes. Move closer to your monitor so the software gets a better view of your face.

If the application runs slowly:
Close heavy background tasks. Games or video editing software consume many system resources. Closing these programs frees up the processor and improves the response time of the gaze marker.
